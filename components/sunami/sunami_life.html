My life in NIT

The most difficult and positive experience of my life at NIT was living in the dormitories.
It was the first time in my life that I lived in a dormitory. When I was a freshman, the rules in the dormitory were very strict, and there were many difficulties. I hated the dorms because I had to be loud every day because of the way I greeted people, and senior students would come to check on me during study time and get angry if I didn't study properly. But now, thanks to this, I feel that I have grown as a person. I feel that I have become a person who can look at things with a relaxed attitude and take things for granted. Since my sophomore year, I have taken responsibility for my own actions in the dormitory, and I have learned to do my laundry and clean my room properly. In addition, I have been able to do my committee work well, and in my fourth year, as the chairperson of the committee, I contributed to organizing the entire committee and making the dormitory a better place to live. I am now able to balance my studies with my life in the dormitory, and I have gained many new experiences in addition to dormitory life, such as studying, writing reports, and doing my graduation research.
